Introduction

About the Program

We begin with Allah's name, seeking His help and His blessing. We praise Him and ask Him to send His peace and blessings on His Messenger, Muhammad ﷺ, and on his family and companions.

This "Shawwal to Sha'ban" program aims to help participants develop a habit of daily engagement with the Qur'an and gain a stronger attachment to Allah's Book. It is our hope that doing so will enable each participant to move to a deeper level of engagement with the Qur'an before next Ramadan.

Between Shawwal and Sha'ban, participants will complete 308 daily reading portions at a level of their choosing.

Reading Levels

Choose Your Level

Pick the engagement level that suits your current relationship with the Qur'an. You may advance or combine levels as the program progresses.

01

Read the Muṣḥaf

(in Arabic)

By completing these small daily reading portions, a person could complete the Qur'an before next Ramadan and develop a habit of daily engagement with Allah's Book.

Suggestions
  • Listen to a reciter before reading your daily portion
  • Recite your daily portion multiple times if possible
  • Read a translation where possible
  • Explore selected explanations at Tulayhah Blog
'Abdullah ibn 'Abbaas said: "Whenever you return from your work or your errands, what is keeping you from leaning back on your couch and reciting a few ayaat from the Qur'an?" (Sunan al-Dārimī #3336)
Most Accessible
02

Read a Translation

(English)

A good translation is both Reliable — faithfully transmitting the messages of the Qur'an as understood by the salaf — and Readable — conveying those messages clearly.

Suggested Translations
  • Sahih International — draws on Tafsir ibn Kathir, Tafsir al-Sa'di, and Tafsir al-Nasafi
  • Hilali-Khan "The Noble Qur'an" — draws on Tafsir al-Tabari, al-Qurtubi, and ibn Kathir with footnotes from Sahih al-Bukhari
Suggestions
  • Keep a notebook for reflections, comments, and questions
  • Try to identify a theme for each day's reading portion
  • Ask yourself: "How does today's reading connect to yesterday's?"
03

Read a Tafsir

(Explanation)

Sheikh 'Abd al-Razzaq al-Badr said: "Something to aid in understanding the Qur'an is the books of tafsir which are relied on by the people of knowledge."

Suggested Books (in English)
  • "The Overall Tafseer of Quran" — from Dorar.net/en/tafseer — basic explanation with useful surah introductions
  • al-Mukhtasar fi Tafsir al-Qur'an al-Karim — introductory level, one page per mushaf page
  • Tafsir al-Sa'di — 10 volumes (IIPH) — easy to read, draws on salaf and ibn al-Qayyim
  • Tafsir ibn Kathir — 10 volumes (Dar-us-Salam) — explains using ayaat, hadith, and salaf statements
Suggestions
  • Keep a notebook to jot down reflections and benefits
  • After reading, summarize the main points
  • Choose one or more benefits each day to record
  • Review your notes weekly
  • After completing a surah, create a summary of themes
  • Ask yourself: "How can I apply this to my own life?"
